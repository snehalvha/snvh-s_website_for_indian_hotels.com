Website Description, Dec 14, 2021

Following are the comforts given in the Indian hotels:

1. Cleanliness: This is an absolute must. Hotels, whether we're talking about a Red Roof Inn or a Bulgari resort, need to uphold the highest cleanliness standards, offering clean public spaces, bathrooms, bedrooms, and amenities.

2. Adequate safety/security: For many, a hotel functions as a home away from home. With that comes a hefty expectation for the most diligent safety and security measures.

3. Internet: Guests at all types of hotels demand some level of InteAdequate safety/security: For many,a hotel functions as a home away from home. With that comes a hefty expectation for the most diligent safety and security measures.

4. Attentive phone answering: Hotels typically have a standard "answer the phone in three rings" policy; unfortunately, this does not happen as often as we would like. Sometimes the call is directed to an agent who does not have the information, but is rather there just to answer the phone…so on to another transfer!

5. Bathroom plumbing: The reason this is highlighted as "plumbing" as opposed to just a "good shower" or "hot water" is because this concerns the entire bathroom.

6. Lighting: This is a pretty big issue across a lot of hotels. Most don't offer adequate lighting in the hotel rooms, from bathrooms to bedside lamps, and it's a hassle for guests across the board.

7. Aroma: Upon entering a hotel and the hotel room, the smell of the property has a major impact on a traveler's first impressions.

8. Simple, tasty food: The availability of food and beverage outlets varies based on the hotel service level. However, certain things—such as a hot breakfast—are a basic offering that really adds to a guest experience.

9. Check in/Check out: Nowadays, a front desk check-in and check-out experience is constantly evolving—from pod check-ins to a personalized iPad check-in. While these advances are exciting, hotels need to stay focused on certain elements of a check-in/check-out process that really effect a guest stay.
